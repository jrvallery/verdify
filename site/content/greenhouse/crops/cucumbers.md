High-water, high-light warm-season crop. Cucumbers are vigorous growers that produce rapidly once established. Good fit for the [[greenhouse/zones/west|West Zone]] with its long wall of shelving and overhead grow lights.

## Optimal Conditions

| Parameter | Range | Notes |
|-----------|-------|-------|
| Day temperature | 75-82°F | Sensitive to cold. Below 60°F = growth stops. |
| Night temperature | 65-70°F | Warmer nights than lettuce |
| VPD | 0.8-1.2 kPa | High transpiration rate — sensitive to dry air |
| DLI | 20-25 mol/m²/d | Heavy light requirement |
| pH (hydro) | 5.5-6.0 | Slightly acidic |
| EC | 1.7-2.5 mS/cm | Moderate-heavy feeder |
| Photoperiod | 14-18 hours | Day-neutral for most greenhouse varieties |

## Varieties for This Greenhouse

| Variety | Type | Length | Days | Pollination | Notes |
|---------|------|--------|------|-------------|-------|
| Beit Alpha | Mini/snacking | 4-6" | 50-55 | Parthenocarpic | **Best for greenhouse** — no pollination needed |
| English (telegraph) | Long | 12-14" | 55-65 | Parthenocarpic | Classic greenhouse cucumber |
| Picolino | Mini | 3-4" | 50-55 | Parthenocarpic | Snacking size, prolific |
| Marketmore | Slicing | 8-9" | 58-68 | Bee-pollinated | Outdoor type, less ideal for greenhouse |
**Recommendation:** Beit Alpha or Picolino. Parthenocarpic varieties are essential in a greenhouse — no bees required.

## Zone Recommendation
**Primary: [[greenhouse/zones/west|West Zone]] shelving or floor**
The west zone offers moderate temperatures with strong afternoon light from the longest wall. Cucumbers need more light than lettuce but less heat tolerance than peppers — the west zone is the sweet spot.
**Hydro option:** Cucumbers can grow in the [[greenhouse/zones/east|East Zone]] hydro system but their vigorous vining habit and large leaves create shading issues for neighboring crops. Better as dedicated plants in the west zone.

## Growing Notes

- **Vertical growing:** Train up a trellis or string to save floor space. A single plant can produce 20+ cucumbers in a season when trained vertically.
- **Water demand:** Cucumbers are 95% water. Consistent, heavy watering essential.
- **Harvest frequently:** Pick cucumbers small for best quality. Overripe fruit signals the plant to stop producing.
- **Sensitive to cold:** More cold-sensitive than tomatoes or peppers. Keep away from the vent opening on cold nights.

## Longmont-Specific Notes

- **Dry air challenge:** Cucumbers are sensitive to low humidity. VPD above 1.5 kPa causes leaf curl. Hot spring afternoons (VPD 2.0+) will stress cucumbers.
- **Short outdoor season:** Longmont's frost-free window (100 days) barely allows one outdoor cucumber crop. The greenhouse provides 3-4 full cycles per year.
